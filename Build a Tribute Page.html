Norman Borlaug timeline of his life:

Norman Borlaug was a renowned American agronomist and humanitarian known for his significant contributions to the Green Revolution and efforts to combat world hunger.

  • March 25, 1914-- Norman Ernest Borlaug was born in Cresco, Iowa, USA.
  • 1937-- Borlaug completed his Bachelor of Science degree in forestry and a Master of Science in plant pathology from the University of Minnesota.
  • 1944-- He received a Ph.D. in plant pathology and genetics from the University of Minnesota.
  • 1944-1960-- Borlaug worked on various agricultural research and development projects, including a wheat improvement program in Mexico.
  • 1950s-- Borlaug and his team developed high-yielding, disease-resistant wheat varieties, leading to the Green Revolution, which significantly increased agricultural productivity in Mexico.
  • 1963-- The success of the Green Revolution in Mexico inspired its expansion to other countries, including India and Pakistan, leading to increased food production and decreased famine.
  • 1970-- Norman Borlaug was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for his contributions to ending world hunger through improved crop varieties and agricultural practices.
  • 1986-- He established the World Food Prize to recognize individuals who have made outstanding contributions to food security.
  • 2009-- Norman Borlaug passed away in Dallas, Texas, leaving behind a lasting legacy in the field of agriculture and hunger alleviation.
  • His work continues to inspire efforts to address global food security and agricultural challenges, and his impact on agriculture and the fight against hunger is widely recognized and celebrated.
